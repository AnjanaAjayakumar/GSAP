Diamond mining

To date, primary diamond deposits mined by surface or underground mining methods on an industrial scale are mainly volcanic pipes, steeply dipping dykes or shallow dipping sills.

Diamonds, which are now easily accessible across the world, were originally found and mined in India, Brazil, South Africa, Botswana, Namibia, Angola and Congo. The world's top three mines are: Botswana (producing 24 million carats of diamonds) Canada (producing 10.9 million carats of diamonds)
